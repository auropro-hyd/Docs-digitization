CHECKLIST REVIEW RULES

Category: Document completeness Checks

1. All raw materials used in manufacturing steps are accounted for in raw material weighing sheet
2. Raw material requisitions are raised and received per the BPCR, and the raw material request cum issue record is attached.
3. All in-process checks (IPC) are performed at the specified manufacturing steps; results recorded in the IPC report match the values entered in the corresponding manufacturing step's Remarks column and comply with BPCR specifications.
4. Packing material requisitions are raised, issued, and received as per the BPCR; the issued quantity matches the requested quantity for each packing material item and the issue record is signed.
5. Correct entries are made for batch start date/time and completion date/time in the manufacturing instructions; the manufacturing date is consistent with the batch start date.
6. For each raw material in the weighing sheet, the dispensed Net Quantity matches the BPCR Standard Quantity; any deviation is explicitly noted or falls within an allowed variable range.
7. All relevant steps are followed as per the BPCR and within standard limits.
8. Material issue and container-wise weight details are correct.

Category: BPCR review checklist
9. "Done by" and "Checked by" signatures are present at all required steps of the BPCR.
10. Is it a pre-validation or validation batch?
11. Any additional operations performed are recorded and the record is attached with the BPCR.
12. Critical process parameters are within the standard range given in the BPCR.
13. Output meets the standard yield range.
14. Correct entries of product name, batch number, batch quantity, AR number, and specification number are present and filled in either the Sample Request to Quality Control form (with number of containers, specification number, and AR number above the "Filled by Quality Control" table) or the Batch Release Note (with product name, batch number, AR number, and quantity).
15. Any change control initiated against this batch is verified in the agile application and the CRF number is recorded.
16. Any deviation initiated for this batch is verified in the agile application and the reference number is recorded.
17. For re-process or rework batches, the OOS/OOT/deviation/market complaint reference number is recorded and verified in the agile application.
18. The batch's relevance to stability or annual addition batch is indicated.
19. Any other remarks (if any) are documented.

--------------------------------------------------

Category: Audit Trail Review

20. Any alarms observed during batch time that exceed predefined timelines as per site SOP are checked and any related deviations or incidents are documented.
21. Any excursions in temperature, vacuum, or process parameters observed beyond predefined timelines as per SOP during batch time are checked and any related deviation or incident is documented.
