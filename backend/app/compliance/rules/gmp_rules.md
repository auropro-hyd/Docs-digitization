GMP VALIDATION RULES

Category: Equipment Identification

1. Equipment IDs are documented for each process step.
2. Equipment IDs are consistent between the BMR and equipment usage logs.
3. Equipment calibration status is referenced or confirmed.
4. Equipment cleaning status is documented before use.
5. Multi-product equipment shows dedicated or validated cleaning procedures.

Category: SOP References

6. Each critical operation references an approved SOP number and revision.
7. SOP references are correctly formatted and verifiable.
8. No outdated or superseded SOP versions are referenced.
9. Operator confirms reading and understanding of applicable SOPs.

Category: Environmental Conditions

10. Environmental monitoring data (temperature, humidity, differential pressure) is recorded where required.
11. Environmental conditions are within validated ranges.
12. Out-of-range environmental readings have documented justification or deviation reports.
13. Clean room classifications are verified before operations begin.

Category: Corrections and Amendments

14. Corrections are made using single-line strikethrough preserving original entry.
15. All corrections include initials and date of the correcting person.
16. Reason for correction is documented when required.
17. No use of correction fluid, erasure, or overwriting.
18. Electronic corrections maintain a full audit trail.

Category: Material Reconciliation

19. Material requisition quantities match dispensing records.
20. Material balance is documented at each process stage.
21. Unexplained discrepancies are investigated with documented rationale.
22. Returned materials are documented with quantities and disposition.

Category: Yield Calculations

23. Theoretical yield is stated for each critical stage.
24. Actual yield is recorded and compared against theoretical yield.
25. Yield deviations outside acceptable limits trigger investigation.
26. Yield calculations are verified by a second person.

Category: In-Process Controls

27. IPC tests are performed at defined intervals per the BMR.
28. IPC results are within validated acceptance criteria.
29. Out-of-specification IPC results are documented and investigated.
30. Sampling procedures follow approved protocols.
31. IPC instruments are calibrated and within validity.
